The trap

{{TRAP_LEAD}} The average customer is a statistical fiction — a blend of people who want opposite things, described so no one in particular is recognizable.

A paragraph on how the client had been treating the market as one audience, and what that costs: messaging aimed at the average lands with no one, because the average is an average of contradictions.

So we stopped averaging and started sorting — and four distinct people fell out of the data, each with a shape of their own.

How we built it

We didn’t guess the segments. We derived them.

01 · Design
Divide, don’t describe

We profile on the attributes that actually split a market — needs, values, behaviours — not the demographics that only describe it.

02 · Field
Sized to survive the cut

A representative sample big enough to hold up once it’s split into pieces — N=1,200 across six markets.

03 · Analyze
Let the groups emerge

A needs-based latent-class model — the segments fall out of the data instead of being drawn where we expected the lines to be.

04 · Validate
Distinct, stable, reachable

Every segment gets pressure-tested: different enough to matter, stable enough to build on, reachable enough to act on.
