Three ways in

We don’t write a recommendation until three separate reads agree.

Quant

N=900 category buyers, sized to detect a five-point gap and no smaller.

Qual

18 switch interviews, with people who had just moved — in either direction.

Behavioural

Three years of transaction data, so nothing rests on what people remember doing.

One recommendation

Written where all three land in the same place — and flagged as a judgement call, out loud, when they don’t.

Then we spend a session arguing the other side: what would have to be true for this recommendation to be wrong, and is any of it true? Whatever survives that gets a confidence level attached, in writing, along with the conditions that would change our mind. A recommendation resting on a single source is a guess with a sample size.
