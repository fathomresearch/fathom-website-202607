Test protocol Filed · day zero
The claim
Written so it can be shown false. “X is true” — never “let’s explore X”, which can only ever come back interesting.
What would sink it
The specific result that kills the claim, named in advance so it can’t be quietly renamed once the data is in.
Evidence
N=800 category buyers to size it, 24 depth interviews to explain it. A claim that survives only one of the two hasn’t survived.
Decision rule
Supported, rejected, or unresolved — read at 95%. Unresolved stays unresolved; a near miss is not promoted.
Anything after
Cuts we run once the data lands are labelled exploratory. They can start the next study. They can’t settle this one.

Pre-registration is routine in clinical work and rare in commercial research. It costs a day. It is the whole difference between running a test and going looking for something flattering.
