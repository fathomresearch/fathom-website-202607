About Fathom
Radical humanity.
Radical authenticity.
Radical honesty.

These aren’t values on a poster. They’re how we actually work — with our clients, with each other, and with the data in front of us.

How we work

Most research firms
make you choose. We don’t.

Everything about how Fathom works comes down to refusing a trade-off that everyone else accepts.

Fast and rigorous

Most firms make you pick one. We built Fathom because that choice is false — speed is a discipline, not a shortcut.

AI-powered and human-led

The machine accelerates the work. The judgment — the so-what, the recommendation — stays ours.

Confident and honest

We stand behind our work with authority — and we tell you plainly what we don’t yet know.

How we hold those together

01 — How we move

Speed is a discipline.

Bias toward progress.

We move when we have enough to move. Research that arrives after the decision is archaeology.

Recommendations, not readouts.

Every engagement ends with a clear point of view on what to do next. The recommendation is the job.

02 — How we grow

The best way isn’t fixed.

We’re all learning.

Heavily invested in training and staying on the cutting edge of new tools and methods.

We experiment constantly.

Always testing new and better ways, because the field keeps moving and so do we.

03 — What we owe you

Honesty and partnership.

Honest about uncertainty.

Nobody can guarantee 100% success. If we don’t know, we say so. No false confidence.

Collaborative by design.

We work alongside your team, not at arm’s length. The conversation goes both ways.

AI & human judgment

AI accelerates.
Humans decide.

We use AI to process, synthesise, and identify patterns at a speed that would otherwise take weeks. What AI cannot do is decide what matters. It cannot understand the strategic context, know which insight will change something for your business, or make a recommendation you can stand behind in a room.

That’s what our researchers do. AI is the accelerator. Human judgment is the engine.

The result: you don’t wait for the analysis. You get straight to the conversation about what to do with it.
